# OctoAcme Project Management Docs

## Overview

OctoAcme adopts a structured and collaborative project management process to deliver customer value predictably. Projects follow a lifecycle of initiation, planning, execution, release, and retrospective, with clear decision gates and measurable success criteria defined from the start. The Project One-pager, risk register, and other lightweight artifacts are used to surface assumptions and keep stakeholders aligned throughout the lifecycle.

Work is organized into prioritized backlog items with explicit acceptance criteria and Definition of Done. Teams use a visual project board that tracks items from Backlog → Ready → In Progress → In Review → QA → Done. Pull requests are intentionally small, include linked issues and acceptance criteria, and must pass CI (tests, linting, security scans) before merging. Risk and dependency management are handled during planning and weekly syncs, with defined escalation paths for blockers.

Roles are clearly defined: Project Managers coordinate schedule, risks, and communications; Product Managers own outcomes and prioritization; Developers implement and test; QA/Testing independently verifies acceptance and quality. These roles collaborate through daily standups, regular planning and demo cadences, and recurring retrospectives that produce tracked action items for continuous improvement.

Communication strategies at OctoAcme focus on transparency and proactive risk management. Regular syncs, status updates, and clear escalation pathways ensure that blockers and incidents are surfaced and resolved quickly. Each project artifact, from the Project Charter to retrospective notes, is versioned and searchable, helping teams onboard quickly and maintain alignment. A strong continuous improvement culture ties it all together, with retrospectives after each release ensuring that learnings translate into actionable improvements.
